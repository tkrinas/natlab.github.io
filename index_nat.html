Welcome

Professor Natassa Pippa serves as an Assistant Professor at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens within the Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences. She is renowned for her work in the field of Pharmaceutical Technology, with a particular focus on developing cutting-edge drug delivery systems and pioneering advancements in nanomedicine. Her research contributes to innovative healthcare solutions, including the design of advanced nanostructures that enhance therapeutic efficacy. Through her extensive work in the development of new medicines, food supplements, and medical devices, she has made significant strides in applying nanotechnology to improve patient care and outcomes in the healthcare industry.

Research Interests

  • Drug Delivery Systems

    Design and development of advanced drug delivery systems including liposomes, polymersomes, micelles, nanoemulsions, dendrimers, and chimeric drug delivery nano systems.

  • Pharmaceutical Technology & Nanotechnology

    Development of innovative dosage forms like liposomes, hydrogels, and multi-compartmentalized systems with applications in drug delivery, nutraceuticals, and medical devices.

  • Protein/Peptide Delivery

    Expertise in protein/peptide loading and controlled release studies, with applications in vaccines and therapeutic proteins.

  • Physicochemical & Thermodynamic Evaluation

    Analysis of excipients, nanomaterials, and drug delivery systems using advanced techniques such as dynamic and static light scattering, freeze-drying, UV-Vis spectroscopy, and more.

  • Quality Assurance & Regulatory Affairs

    Preparation of technical dossiers for pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and cosmetics. Lead auditor for ISO 9001, ISO 13485, and ISO 22000 standards.
